# Writing Guidelines to Reduce AI-Like Writing Patterns

## Purpose

The goal of this document is to help produce website content that reads naturally, reflects genuine human communication, and avoids stylistic patterns that are commonly associated with AI-generated text.

These guidelines are intended to improve authenticity, readability, and user trust—not to deceive readers about authorship.

---

# General Principles

When writing content for the website:

* Write for people first, not algorithms.
* Prioritize clarity over perfection.
* Allow the writing to sound natural instead of mechanically polished.
* Avoid overly predictable sentence structures.
* Vary rhythm, sentence length, and paragraph length.
* Prefer specific observations over generic statements.

---

# Avoid Common AI Writing Patterns

## 1. Do Not Overuse Transitional Words

Avoid excessive repetition of transitions such as:

* Furthermore
* Moreover
* Additionally
* Therefore
* Consequently
* Nevertheless
* Overall
* Ultimately
* In conclusion

Transitions should only appear when they genuinely improve clarity.

---

## 2. Avoid Formulaic Introductions

Do not begin articles with generic openings such as:

* In today's rapidly evolving world...
* As technology continues to evolve...
* It is undeniable that...
* It is important to note that...
* There is no doubt that...

Instead, start with a concrete idea, a real problem, a question, or an observation that immediately provides value.

---

## 3. Avoid Formulaic Conclusions

Do not automatically end every page with:

* In conclusion...
* Overall...
* Ultimately...
* To summarize...

Many pages do not require a formal conclusion.

If the content naturally ends after delivering its message, stop there.

---

## 4. Reduce Repetition

Do not repeat the same idea using different wording simply to increase length.

Every paragraph should introduce new information.

Remove sentences that merely restate previous content.

---

## 5. Vary Sentence Length

Avoid producing paragraphs where every sentence has approximately the same length.

Instead, combine:

* short sentences
* medium-length sentences
* longer explanatory sentences

Natural variation improves readability.

---

## 6. Vary Paragraph Length

Do not create pages where every paragraph contains exactly three or four sentences.

Mix:

* short paragraphs
* medium paragraphs
* occasional longer explanations when necessary

---

## 7. Avoid Excessive Neutrality

Do not present every topic with perfectly balanced pros and cons unless balance is actually required.

Instead:

* make clear recommendations when appropriate
* explain why one approach may be preferable
* support statements with reasoning

---

## 8. Prefer Specific Details

Replace generic language with concrete information.

Avoid:

> Our platform offers innovative solutions.

Prefer:

> Our platform automates invoice reconciliation, synchronizes accounting data with ERP systems, and reduces manual processing time.

Specificity creates credibility.

---

## 9. Avoid Generic Marketing Language

Minimize expressions such as:

* cutting-edge
* revolutionary
* innovative
* game-changing
* world-class
* industry-leading

Only use these terms when they are supported by measurable evidence.

---

## 10. Use Natural Vocabulary

Do not maintain exactly the same writing style throughout an entire article.

Natural writing includes variation in:

* vocabulary
* sentence openings
* phrasing
* emphasis

---

## 11. Remove Filler

Avoid unnecessary expressions such as:

* It is worth noting that...
* It is important to recognize that...
* One key aspect is...
* Another important factor is...
* From this perspective...

If removing the phrase does not change the meaning, delete it.

---

## 12. Prefer Active Voice

Instead of:

> The report was generated by the system.

Prefer:

> The system generated the report.

Active voice is usually more direct and easier to read.

---

## 13. Use Concrete Examples

Whenever possible, illustrate ideas with realistic examples, scenarios, or practical applications.

Examples make content feel more authentic and useful.

---

## 14. Avoid Excessive Symmetry

Do not force every section to have identical structure.

For example, avoid writing every subsection as:

* Definition
* Benefits
* Challenges
* Conclusion

Natural writing allows sections to expand or contract based on the subject.

---

## 15. Eliminate Predictable Patterns

Review the draft and remove repetitive habits such as:

* starting multiple sentences with the same word
* repeating identical grammatical structures
* overusing lists
* repeating the same transition words

Variation improves the reading experience.

---

# Editorial Checklist

Before publishing, verify that the content satisfies the following:

* The introduction is specific and engaging.
* Transitional words are used sparingly.
* No unnecessary concluding paragraph has been added.
* Sentences vary in length.
* Paragraphs vary in length.
* Repetition has been removed.
* Marketing clichés have been minimized.
* Claims are supported by concrete information.
* Active voice is used whenever appropriate.
* Examples are included where they improve understanding.
* The writing sounds natural rather than mechanically perfect.

---

# Objective

The objective is not to imitate imperfection but to produce writing that is clear, informative, trustworthy, and naturally engaging.

High-quality website content should prioritize precision, authenticity, and usefulness over rigid formatting or predictable stylistic patterns.
